FAQ

You asked, we answered.

Not at all! Base rates in DeFi — the rate borrowers pay, minus protocol reserves — are often enhanced with additional rewards paid in other tokens. You can either hold those tokens if you think their price will appreciate, or quickly and easily convert them back to USDC (or whatever asset you want to earn) to maximize compounding.

Yield comes from borrowers who pay interest to access liquidity. When you supply assets to a lending market, borrowers pay a variable rate that fluctuates based on supply and demand. That interest flows directly to you as a supplier.

DeFi lending rates are variable and change continuously based on market utilization. When demand to borrow increases, rates go up. When it decreases, rates go down. This is normal and transparent — you can always see the current rate in real time.

DeFi protocols are not FDIC-insured, but they are secured by smart contracts that have been extensively audited by leading security firms. Protocols like Aave also maintain safety modules and bug bounty programs as additional layers of protection.

There is no minimum deposit required. You can start earning with any amount.
